Overview

This project involved us coming up with a new character idea from scratch, creating illustrations, sculpting and eventually texturing and rendering. This was an iterative process where, at every step, we would try new things, get feedback, adjust and repeat.

This class had us using Photoshop, Adobe Substance Painter, Unreal Engine, and (most difficult of all) ZBrush. We had the opporunity to familiarize ourselves with these softwares and use them to achieve a vision of a character we created at the beginning of the semester.

Initial Design

During the first part of the class, we created illustrations for how the character would look. I went through a few iterations before I landed on a specific look

One-Sheet

Once the final details were decided, I created a "one-sheet", which demonstrates how the character is supposed to look from a neutral pose and includes details to help the artist create the 3D model.

Modeling

With the concept developed, I went into ZBrush and went through the process of sculpting.

Texturing

After the model was created, the model was optimized for adding textures. Since I had picked a stylized look, I elected to give more of a painterly feel to my model by painting my textures onto my model by hand.

This is also where the different materials stop looking like clay and gain properties such as looking like shiny metal or having a bright glow.
