Overview

This project involved us coming up with a new idea for a game environment from scratch, creating illustrations, modeling and eventually texturing and rendering. This was an iterative process where, at every step, we would try new things, get feedback, adjust and repeat.

This class had us using Photoshop, Adobe Substance Painter, Unreal Engine, and (most difficult of all) Maya. We had the opporunity to familiarize ourselves with these softwares and use them to achieve a vision of a environment we created at the beginning of the semester.

Greyboxing

Greyboxing started in Unreal, where the basic shapes provided in the program allowed me to lay out proportions and how I wanted the scene to be arranged generally.

Modeling

After I knew the general shape of what I wanted, I went into the modeling software Maya to create the mesh that would take the place of the greyboxes

Texturing

After the models were created, I painted textures onto the surface to give it more color than just grey. In order to accomplish this, the model has to be cut apart and laid out flat so the texture program knows where to put the image files on the model.

Lighting/Wrapping Up

I set up the rest of the textures and models in the scene. At this point, lighting and composition became the main focus.
